Welcome

The goal of this page is to introduce myself and highlight my work as a graduate student in the field of organizational communication.
Through sharing a bit of myself and my experience with graduate work, I hope to demystify the world of academia and encourage people to consider academic work--whether it be reading more academic articles or pursuing a graduate degree of their own. Though the primary focus will be on my work, in the future I plan to share more stories about my academic journey.

About Me

Currently, I am a first year doctoral student at the University of Texas at Austin. My research interests focus on the area of organizational communication research. Within this area of scholarship, my research questions typically revolve around the following areas:

  • Communicating character
  • Organizational hiring and selection
  • Storytelling
  • Impression management

    • Outside of research, I also love advising and teaching in both academic and industry settings. Additionally, I am passionate about providing mentorship to anyone figuring out their career path. My main approach to advising is to ask people the tough questions that encourage self-awareness, promote self-care, and empower them to contribute to their communities in a way that best utilizes their skills, talents, and passions.
